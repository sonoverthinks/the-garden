# The Wood Wide Web

Beneath every step you take in a forest lies a complex, thriving network of fungal threads known as **mycelium**. These networks form symbiotic relationships with tree roots, creating a biological internet that allows plants to communicate and share resources.

## 1. Symbiotic Exchange
Mycorrhizal fungi trade phosphorus and nitrogen for the carbon-rich sugars produced by trees through photosynthesis. It is a massive, decentralized global trade network.

## 2. Chemical Signaling
When a tree is attacked by aphids or drought, it can send chemical distress signals through the mycelial network. Neighboring trees receive these "emails" and begin producing defensive compounds before the threat even reaches them.

## 3. The Mother Tree
Research suggests that "Mother Trees" use these networks to recognize their kin, sending them extra nutrients to ensure their survival in the shaded understory.

### Key Components
- **Hyphae:** The individual microscopic threads.
- **Mycelium:** The mass of branching hyphae.
- **Mycorrhizae:** The specific symbiotic association between fungus and root.
